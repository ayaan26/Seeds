Starting with Zara’s “people” rating, i.e its labour practices, it is below average. A big chunk of its final stages of production takes place in Spain which is known to be a medium risk country in terms of exploiting factory workers. In terms of transparency, Zara ranks about 50%. They provide a detailed list of suppliers and policies. However, there is not much evidence that Zara ensures adequate pay for all workers in its supply chain. Plus, information on COVID-19 policies remain minimal.

Zara’s treatment of animals also recieves below average ratings since they use leather, down, and often enough, exotic animal fur.

Planet ratings, are also unfortunately below average. They do not take the effort in using eco-friendly materials and they do not show efforts in minimizing waste during production. They have objectives to reduce greenhouse gases, but no evidence has been provided. They do, however use recycable packaging.
