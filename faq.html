Sustainability means meeting our individual and collective needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs. Essentially, using resources wisely.


The main goal for seeds is to provide consumers with a comprehensive list of companies with information about their initiatives to take care of the world, fellow human beings, and animals. Hopefully, inspiring them to make more informed decisions and showing people it is possible to consume ethically.

There are three main issues with the fashion industry:
In terms of environment, the industry accounts for 10% of global carbon emissions. The industry generates huge amounts of greenhouse gases during its production, manufacturing, and transportation of the millions garments created every year.
With regards to labour issues, the fashion supply chain is teeming with underpaid, overworked, and underaged employees, including forced and trafficked labour. Many companies, in the pursuit of profit, forget to value the lives on the people who work for them.
Finally, the treatment of animals in the fashion industry is a major concern. For example, animals used to produce leather do not have the same legal protection as pets, which often makes them subject to painful procedures and other types of abuse.


Our rating system collects public information and scores each brand on how they impact people, animals, and the planet, through their labour practices, environmental and social initiatives. Our primary data sources consist of leading indicators of sustainability and industry best practices including certifications, accreditations, standards, and additional public reporting by brands and their parent companies.
