Adidas compared to its competitors,
including Nike, Puma,and New Balance
Adidas is miles ahead in terms of
sustainability and labour conditions.

Adidas "people" category receives low ratings. There are have been reports of mistreatments regarding unfair wages, hours worked and poor working conditions. Adidas must take responsibility for this unacceptable abuse of workers’ basic dignity. All workers must be able to work reasonable hours, free from the fear of harassment and abuse. Adidas must act to ensure that this is not allowed anywhere in their global supply chains.Compared to other similar brands they often rank higher.

Adidas “animals” category slightly above average ratings. Adidas prohibits the use of leathers, hides or skins from animals that have been inhumanely treated, whether these animals are wild or farmed. It does not use fur, exotic animal skin, exotic animal hair, or angora.

Planet ratings are average. Adidas uses a medium proportion of eco-friendly materials including recycled materials, and 5 years ago, it started partnering with ocean conservation group Parley for the Oceans to produce a range of products made from recycled waste from the sea.
